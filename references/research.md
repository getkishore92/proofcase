# Research basis

Hiring practices change. Check current sources before using time-sensitive claims in public copy.

## Durable findings

### Build around comparable evidence

Jared Spool's 2025 material on tailored case studies argues that hiring managers want direct evidence that a candidate has done comparable work. It recommends selecting requirements from the job ad, telling the relevant story, choosing visuals that support it, and writing the account so it works without the candidate present.

Source: [Job Hunt: Tricks for Creating Tailored Case Studies](https://uie.com/share/pdf/a0c6487c-32ec-4e4f-9821-8f3cd45d6bd8_2025-06-09_-_Job_Hunt_Tricks_for_Creating_Tailored_Case_Studies.pdf), UIE, 2025.

### Resist formulaic process theater

Fabricio Teixeira's essay questions the repeated formula used in junior UX case studies and the way templates can replace critical thinking. Use project-specific decisions and evidence instead of mandatory process sections.

Source: [The case study factory](https://essays.uxdesign.cc/case-study-factory/), UX Collective.

### Make the work easy to inspect

Current portfolio commentary from hiring managers and design directors repeatedly favors clear role attribution, strong visual hierarchy, visible final work, concise writing, and project-specific decisions. These reports are qualitative and sometimes disagree on the balance between craft and process. The safe synthesis is to support a fast scan, then make deeper evidence available.

Sources: [The Portfolio Hiring Managers Want to See](https://careers.heb.com/blog/the-portfolio-hiring-managers-want-to-see), H-E-B, 2025; [15 brilliant design portfolio examples, and why they work](https://www.creativebloq.com/portfolios/examples-712368), Creative Bloq, 2026; [How to Evaluate a Product Designer's Portfolio](https://designpixil.com/blog/how-to-evaluate-designer-portfolio), Design Pixil.

### Match the portfolio to the role and level

NN/g's UX careers research distinguishes portfolio expectations across UX disciplines and discusses what hiring teams need from design and research evidence. Use the role, scope, and audience to decide what belongs.

Source: [User Experience Careers, 2nd Edition](https://media.nngroup.com/media/reports/free/UserExperienceCareers_2nd_Edition.pdf), Nielsen Norman Group.

## AI-era implications

### Skills evidence receives more attention

LinkedIn's 2025 Future of Recruiting research surveyed 1,271 recruiting professionals across 23 countries. Ninety-three percent said accurate skill assessment is important to quality of hire, and teams using skills-based searches were more likely to report quality hires. A portfolio case study can serve as inspectable skills evidence when it shows decisions, artifacts, ownership, and results.

Source: [How AI Will Redefine Recruiting in 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/business/talent/blog/talent-acquisition/future-of-recruiting-2025), LinkedIn, 2025.

### AI literacy is growing, alongside human judgment

LinkedIn's 2025 skills analysis lists AI literacy among the fastest-growing skills in several countries. Strategic thinking, communication, adaptability, problem-solving, and code review also appear. A useful case study shows AI fluency through real work while preserving evidence of judgment, collaboration, and verification.

Source: [Skills on the Rise in 2025](https://www.linkedin.com/business/talent/blog/learning-and-development/skills-on-the-rise), LinkedIn, 2025.

Adobe's hiring research lists adaptive problem-solving, collaboration, ethical AI oversight, and creative intuition among skills hiring managers prioritize in the age of AI. Treat this as broad workforce evidence, not a product-design-specific hiring rule.

Source: [Creative Skills Roadmap: Closing the AI Hiring Gap](https://www.adobe.com/acrobat/resources/ai-creative-skills-roadmap.html), Adobe.

## Working conclusion

Case studies remain useful as proof and interview setup. AI can produce polished screens, process diagrams, and fluent prose quickly, so polish alone carries less information about authorship or judgment. Strong case studies make contribution traceable, connect evidence to decisions, show shipped or testable work, and explain how the candidate used and checked AI where relevant.

Do not claim that every hiring manager reads full case studies or requires AI work. Practices vary by company, discipline, and stage. Design for a fast scan, then provide defensible depth.

The same caution applies to claims about motion, interactive portfolios, and easter eggs. They can show craft, builder ability, and personality. They do not repair weak ownership or evidence, and some reviewers prefer restrained static work. Use them when they reveal the project or the candidate's capability.

## Related public skills checked

- [portfolio-audit](https://github.com/hey-stefan/portfolio-audit) audits a complete design portfolio with strong emphasis on visual craft and builder signals.
- [grill-me](https://github.com/julianoczkowski/designer-skills/tree/main/grill-me) interviews a user to stress-test a plan or design.
- [Anthropic skills](https://github.com/anthropics/skills) provides general examples and the Agent Skills format.

These overlap with site critique, questioning, or skill packaging. They do not provide the same end-to-end case-study reconstruction, evidence ledger, seniority calibration, job tailoring, and interview-defense workflow.
